Antoine Wartner is a French junior graphic designer who was born and raised in Massy, France. In 2023, he achieved his Master's degree from the Communication Graphique atelier at HEAR Strasbourg. This accomplishment followed his completion of a Bachelor's degree within the same atelier in 2021. During that same year, he participated in an Erasmus program for his Bachelor's degree at the GDA Graphic Design Atelier of ArtEZ University of the Arts. Antoine also had the opportunity to delve into the realm of printed graphic design through a 2-year degree program at LTAA Auguste Renoir between 2018 and 2020.
Antoine expertise encompasses a wide array of domains within the field of graphic design, including book design, typography, visual identity, photography, and interactive projects. He take great pride in his approach to design, always striving to deliver projects that are both aesthetically pleasing and highly functional. Antoine dissects the relationship between transcription and visual design, uncovering the impact of language on the aesthetics and functionality of his projects.

[Type Design]

Eymet Sans Sténo

2023

MA Project

The development of EYMET SANS is based on a phonetic alphabet and an OpenType ligature system to reproduce the composition of stenograms, following the French Duployé method an archive documents printed in 1912. The extensive nature of the project calls for a dictionary or glossary of the used terms, along with a synthesis of the conversations.

Born from a serie of 6 interviews with ancient short-hand writters, this project takes a look at writing and aspects surrounding it to allow us to question the value of a graphic system, to understand obsolescence, to go through a history of methods. It explores the heritage of a graphic system blown away by the quest for ultimate speed.

[Prototyping][Instalation]

QWARE_2

2023

MA Project

Development of a device for real-time tracking of position points on the x, y, and z axes. QWARE camera diversion, enabling tracking for TikTok content creation. 3D-printed piece with an integrated drawing axis, articulated by a ball bearing and threaded rod motor system controlled by an Arduino. Short intervals of back-and-forth movement every 35 seconds in a loop.

This tool generates circular maps that represent the movements of subjects within a predefined space. The tracking system activation chip relies on a downloadable application available on all app stores.

15x8x25cm

Arduino programing

Qware Capture tracking camera


Hear, Communication graphique Tutoring by André Bihler & Kevin Donnot

*Chiro--Graphisme*

2022

MA Thesis

Memory conducted as part of the DNSEP at HEAR, Graphic Language workshop, discussing the process of transcription within a graphic design approach.
[First part] What is *Chiro--Graphisme*. A chronological approach to transcription, from the origins of conversational analysis to the emergence of mediated (online) interaction, showcasing projects that intertwine transcription and form construction design.
[Second part] Conversation transcription featuring Frank Leibovici, Yaël Kreplak, and Matias Tripodi. Is transcribing conversational information still relevant today?

18x10 cm 108 pages

4 exemplaires

Coptic binding

Lumière Lana 90g


Hear, communication graphique Tutoring Nina Støttrup Larsen & Lidwine Prolonge

[Identity][Motion Design][Ai]

Fin de la grève de la figure

2022

HEAR

Global identity design for the exhibition of the artist Tudi Deligne across multiple platforms, held at La Chaufferie, Strasbourg.

Tudi Deligne draws inspiration from his vast image bank, built over several years, comprising over 5GB of images and GIFs of various kinds. Through training and utilizing GAN (Generative Adversarial Networks) in collaboration with the artist, patterns are generated from these 5000 reference images. This process is used to create poster backgrounds, abstract compositions, and motion animations for exhibition visuals.
